This research session provided a comprehensive tech stack recommendation tailored for a pre-seed cross-border B2B payments startup with a $500K budget.

### **Final Recommendation Summary**

To maximize your runway while ensuring compliance and scalability, adopt an **API-first, modular architecture**. Avoid building complex infrastructure from scratch; instead, integrate specialized, cost-effective vendors that you can swap out as your transaction volume and regulatory footprint grow.

*   **Infrastructure**: Utilize **AWS Activate** or **Google for Startups** credits to cover cloud infrastructure costs. Use managed services (e.g., Managed PostgreSQL) to reduce operational headcount.
*   **Backend**: Use **Python (FastAPI or Django)**. It is the standard for high-performance, maintainable fintech backends.
*   **Compliance (KYC/AML)**: Integrate an all-in-one, API-first compliance platform like **Flagright** or **Sumsub**. These allow for rapid deployment and easy updates to risk rules without engineering overhaul.
*   **Real-time FX**: Use cost-effective, reliable APIs like **ExchangeRate-API** or **Fixer.io**.
*   **Payment Rails**: Leverage mature payment aggregators (e.g., **Stripe**, **Nium**) initially to handle the underlying cross-border movement, avoiding the extreme cost and complexity of building your own banking integrations at the pre-seed stage.

Your priority should be **proving your business model** with a thin, scalable layer of technology that satisfies immediate regulatory requirements.
